## Patient Communication

1. `Write a patient-friendly explanation of [medical condition]. Reading level: 6th grade. Include: what it is, symptoms, how it's diagnosed, treatment options, lifestyle changes, and when to seek emergency care. Avoid medical jargon.`
2. `Create a pre-procedure information sheet for [procedure]. Include: what to expect before/during/after, preparation instructions (fasting, medications), risks and benefits, recovery timeline, and frequently asked questions.`
3. `Draft a discharge summary in patient-friendly language for [condition/procedure]. Include: what was done, medications (name, dose, schedule, side effects), follow-up appointments, warning signs to watch for, activity restrictions.`
4. `Write a medication counseling script for [medication]. Cover: what it does, how to take it, common side effects, serious side effects (seek help), food/drug interactions, storage, and missed dose instructions.`
5. `Create a chronic disease self-management guide for [diabetes/hypertension/asthma/COPD]. Include: daily monitoring, medication adherence, diet guidelines, exercise recommendations, symptom tracking, and when to call the doctor.`
6. `Draft a telehealth visit preparation guide for patients. Include: tech setup, information to have ready, how to describe symptoms effectively, questions to ask, and follow-up expectations.`
7. `Write a vaccination information sheet for [vaccine]. Include: what diseases it prevents, who should get it, schedule, common reactions, rare adverse events, and contraindications. Follow CDC VIS format.`
8. `Create a mental health resource guide for [anxiety/depression/PTSD]. Include: recognizing symptoms, self-help strategies, when to seek help, types of therapy, medication overview, crisis resources.`
9. `Draft a surgical consent discussion guide. Cover: diagnosis, proposed procedure, alternatives (including no treatment), risks, benefits, expected outcomes, recovery, and opportunity for questions.`
10. `Write a pediatric health milestone guide for parents. Age ranges: 0-12 months, 1-3 years, 3-5 years. For each: physical, cognitive, social-emotional milestones. Include: when to express concerns, screening schedule.`

## Clinical Documentation

11. `Create a clinical note template (SOAP format) for [specialty]. Subjective: HPI, ROS, PMH, medications, allergies. Objective: vitals, physical exam findings. Assessment: diagnosis, differential. Plan: treatment, follow-up, patient education.`
12. `Write a referral letter to [specialist] for a patient with [condition]. Include: reason for referral, relevant history, current medications, diagnostic results, specific question for the specialist.`
13. `Draft a peer-reviewed journal article structured abstract (250 words) for a clinical study on [topic]. Sections: Background, Methods, Results, Conclusions. Follow CONSORT/STROBE guidelines as applicable.`
14. `Create a case presentation template for [specialty] morning report. Format: chief complaint, HPI (timeline), PMH/medications/allergies, social history, physical exam, labs/imaging, differential diagnosis (most likely to least), assessment and plan.`
15. `Write a clinical pathway for managing [condition] in the emergency department. Decision tree: triage ‚Üí initial workup ‚Üí risk stratification ‚Üí treatment ‚Üí disposition (admit/discharge). Include: evidence-based timeframes.`

## Medical Education

16. `Create a study guide for [medical topic] suitable for medical students. Include: key concepts, pathophysiology flowchart description, clinical presentation, diagnostic criteria, first-line treatment, complications, and 10 practice questions.`
17. `Write 20 USMLE-style multiple choice questions on [topic]. Each: clinical vignette, 5 answer choices, correct answer, and explanation for why each distractor is wrong. Mix difficulty levels.`
18. `Design a simulation scenario for [emergency procedure/condition]. Include: patient demographics, presenting complaint, vitals progression, lab values, imaging findings, expected interventions, critical actions checklist, and debriefing questions.`
19. `Create a clinical reasoning exercise for [case]. Format: present information sequentially, ask "what's your differential?" at each stage, reveal additional data, narrow differential. Final: diagnosis and management plan.`
20. `Write a pharmacology review for [drug class]. For each drug: mechanism of action, indications, contraindications, side effects, monitoring parameters, drug interactions, and clinical pearls.`
21. `Develop an anatomy review using clinical correlations for [body region]. Key structures, clinical significance of each, common pathologies, physical exam techniques, and imaging landmarks.`
22. `Create an evidence-based medicine (EBM) exercise. Given: [journal article summary]. Tasks: identify study design, assess validity (internal/external), calculate NNT/NNH, apply to patient scenario, grade evidence.`
23. `Write a differential diagnosis framework for [symptom]. Organized by: life-threatening (must rule out first) ‚Üí common ‚Üí less common ‚Üí rare. Include: key distinguishing features and initial workup for each.`
24. `Design a clinical skills assessment checklist for [procedure: intubation, central line, lumbar puncture, etc.]. Steps: indication verification, consent, equipment, positioning, technique, confirmation, documentation. Pass/fail criteria.`
25. `Create a radiology teaching file for [imaging finding]. Include: modality, clinical context, key findings, differential diagnosis, recommended next steps, and classic teaching point.`

## Healthcare Operations

26. `Design a patient flow optimization plan for [ED/clinic/OR]. Analyze: current bottlenecks, wait times, throughput, patient satisfaction. Propose: process improvements, staffing adjustments, technology solutions.`
27. `Create a quality improvement (QI) project proposal using PDSA methodology. Topic: [quality issue]. Plan: aim statement, measures (outcome, process, balancing), change ideas. Do: small test implementation. Study: data analysis. Act: scale or modify.`
28. `Write a root cause analysis (RCA) for a [patient safety event]. Format: event description, timeline, contributing factors (human, system, equipment, environment), root causes, corrective actions, monitoring plan.`
29. `Design a clinical dashboard for [department/unit]. Metrics: patient volume, wait times, length of stay, mortality rate, readmission rate, patient satisfaction, staff-to-patient ratio, hand hygiene compliance.`
30. `Create a hospital emergency preparedness plan. Scenarios: mass casualty, pandemic surge, natural disaster, cybersecurity attack. For each: command structure (ICS), resource allocation, communication, and deactivation criteria.`

## Research & Clinical Trials

37. `Create an informed consent form for a clinical trial. Include: purpose, procedures, duration, risks, benefits, alternatives, compensation, confidentiality, voluntary participation, contact information. Reading level: 8th grade.`
38. `Design a clinical trial recruitment strategy for [study population]. Channels: provider referral, patient registry, social media, community outreach. Inclusion/exclusion screening workflow. Diversity and inclusion plan.`
39. `Write a Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) charter. Include: composition, responsibilities, meeting schedule, interim analysis plan, stopping rules, conflict of interest, and reporting structure.`
40. `Create a clinical trial database design for [study]. Tables: subjects, visits, adverse events, lab results, medications. Fields for each. Data validation rules. Query resolution workflow.`
