## Ideation & Validation

1. `I have a startup idea: [idea]. Act as a YC partner during office hours. Poke holes in my idea. Ask me the 5 hardest questions a skeptical investor would ask. Be brutally honest about what could kill this company.`
2. `Generate 20 micro-SaaS ideas targeting [industry/role]. For each: problem statement, solution, target customer, pricing model, competitive moat, and estimated TAM. Focus on problems worth paying monthly for.`
3. `Create a lean validation plan for [startup idea]. Include: 10 customer interview questions, landing page copy, MVP scope (build in 2 weeks), success metrics, and kill criteria (when to pivot).`
4. `Analyze the competitive landscape for [market]. Create a 5-forces analysis (Porter's): threat of new entrants, bargaining power of suppliers, bargaining power of buyers, threat of substitutes, competitive rivalry. Include specific company names.`
5. `Write 10 customer discovery interview questions for [product idea]. Questions that uncover: current pain points, existing solutions, willingness to pay, switching costs, decision-making process. No leading questions.`
6. `I want to validate [idea] without writing any code. Design 3 no-code validation experiments ranked by speed: smoke test (48 hours), concierge MVP (1 week), wizard-of-oz MVP (2 weeks).`
7. `Create a TAM/SAM/SOM analysis for [market]. Total Addressable Market, Serviceable Addressable Market, Serviceable Obtainable Market. Include: data sources, assumptions, and calculation methodology.`
8. `Evaluate my startup idea using the "Idea Maze" framework. Map out: why this idea is being attempted now, what previous attempts failed and why, what's changed (technology, regulation, behavior), and the non-obvious path to success.`
9. `Generate problem-solution fit hypotheses for [customer segment]. List 10 specific problems, rank by: severity (1-10), frequency (daily/weekly/monthly), existing alternatives, willingness to pay.`
10. `Run a pre-mortem analysis for [startup idea]. Imagine it's 18 months from now and the startup has failed. What are the 10 most likely reasons for failure? For each, what's the leading indicator we should monitor?`

## Business Planning
