## Literature Review & Research

1. `Conduct a systematic literature review on "[topic]". Identify: 5 seminal papers, 10 recent papers (2020-2025), key themes, methodological trends, research gaps, and conflicting findings. Format as an annotated bibliography.`
2. `Analyze the research methodology of this study: "[study description]". Evaluate: design type (experimental, observational, mixed), sampling strategy, data collection, analysis methods, validity threats, and generalizability.`
3. `Compare and contrast [Theory A] and [Theory B] in the context of [field]. For each: key proponents, core assumptions, supporting evidence, criticisms, and applicability to [specific problem]. Present as a structured comparison table.`
4. `Identify research gaps in [field/topic]. Analyze: what's been studied (themes), how (methods), where (geographic focus), who (populations), and when (time periods). Highlight: underexplored questions, methodological limitations, populations, and contexts.`
5. `Write an abstract for a research paper on "[topic]". Follow IMRaD structure: background (2 sentences), methods (2 sentences), results (2 sentences), discussion/implications (2 sentences). Under 250 words.`
6. `Create a research proposal outline for "[topic]". Sections: introduction, literature review, research questions (3), methodology, expected contributions, timeline (12 months), budget estimate, ethical considerations.`
7. `Develop a theoretical framework for studying [phenomenon]. Identify: relevant theories, key variables, hypothesized relationships, mediating/moderating factors, and boundary conditions. Include a visual model description.`
8. `Write a critical analysis of [research paper/article]. Evaluate: argument strength, evidence quality, logical consistency, methodological rigor, alternative explanations, implications, and contribution to the field.`
9. `Generate 15 research questions about [topic]. Categorize by type: descriptive (5), relational (5), and causal (5). Rate each by: novelty, feasibility, and potential impact. Recommend the top 3 to pursue.`
10. `Create a concept map for [research area]. Central concept â†’ 5 primary concepts â†’ 3 sub-concepts each â†’ key relationships between them. Include: definitions, key researchers, and seminal works.`

## Academic Writing

11. `Write a compelling research paper introduction for "[topic]". Structure: broad context â†’ narrow focus â†’ gap in knowledge â†’ research question â†’ significance â†’ paper overview. 500 words, academic tone.`
12. `Draft a methods section for a [type] study on [topic]. Include: research design, participants/sample, procedures, instruments/measures, data analysis plan, ethical approval, and limitations of the approach.`
13. `Write a discussion section interpreting these findings: [findings]. Structure: restate key results â†’ relate to literature â†’ explain unexpected results â†’ theoretical implications â†’ practical implications â†’ limitations â†’ future research.`
14. `Create a results section for [data type]. Present: descriptive statistics, inferential tests, effect sizes, confidence intervals, and visual descriptions (table/figure references). Use APA 7th edition formatting.`
15. `Write a conclusion for a research paper on [topic]. Summarize: main findings, theoretical contribution, practical implications, limitations, and 3 specific directions for future research. Avoid introducing new information.`
16. `Draft a cover letter for journal submission to [journal name]. Include: manuscript title, brief summary, why this journal is appropriate, confirmation of originality, conflict of interest disclosure, suggested reviewers (3).`
17. `Write a response to peer reviewers for [paper title]. Address each comment: thank the reviewer, explain changes made, provide rationale for respectful disagreement, reference specific page/line numbers.`
18. `Create an academic blog post summarizing [research paper] for a general audience. Translate jargon, use analogies, lead with the "so what?", include practical implications, link to the full paper. 800 words.`
19. `Write thesis chapter outlines for a [Master's/PhD] on [topic]. 5-6 chapters: introduction, literature review, methodology, results, discussion, conclusion. Each: purpose, key sections, estimated word count.`
20. `Draft a conference presentation abstract (300 words) for a paper on [topic]. Hook the selection committee: clear research question, novel method/finding, significance, and implications.`

## Data Analysis & Statistics

21. `Recommend the appropriate statistical test for: [describe data and research question]. Consider: variable types (continuous, categorical, ordinal), number of groups, sample size, distribution assumptions, independence.`
22. `Explain how to interpret [statistical output]: include p-value, confidence interval, effect size (Cohen's d, Î·Â², rÂ²), and what each means in practical terms. Write for a researcher who understands basics but isn't a statistician.`
23. `Design a mixed-methods research study on [topic]. Quantitative: survey design, sample size calculation, analysis plan. Qualitative: interview protocol, coding strategy, and thematic analysis. Integration: how qual and quant combine.`
24. `Create a codebook for qualitative analysis of [data type]. Define: 15 codes organized in 4 themes. For each code: name, definition, inclusion criteria, exclusion criteria, and example quote.`
25. `Write a power analysis justification for a study on [topic]. Using [test type]: effect size (small/medium/large), alpha level, desired power, resulting minimum sample size. Include reference for expected effect size.`
26. `Design a survey instrument for measuring [construct]. Include: 20 items across 4 dimensions, response scale (Likert 1-7), reverse-coded items (4), demographic questions (5), and administration instructions.`
27. `Explain the difference between: correlation and causation, Type I and Type II errors, parametric and non-parametric tests, cross-sectional and longitudinal designs, internal and external validity. Use concrete examples.`
28. `Create a data analysis plan for [study type]. Pre-registration format: hypotheses, variables, analysis methods, assumption checks, handling of missing data, sensitivity analyses, and multiple comparison corrections.`
29. `Generate a synthetic dataset description for testing [analysis method]. Variables: 5 independent, 1 dependent, 2 confounders. Include: distributions, relationships, sample size, missing data pattern (MCAR/MAR/MNAR).`
30. `Write a results narrative for these statistical findings: [insert findings]. Translate numbers into meaningful statements. Explain practical significance, not just statistical significance.`

32. `Create a specific aims page for [NIH/NSF-style grant]. Long-term goal, objective of this project, central hypothesis, 3 specific aims with rationale, significance, and innovation statements.`

## Research Ethics

41. `Write an IRB/ethics application for a study involving [participants/method]. Include: study purpose, procedures, risks and benefits, informed consent process, confidentiality measures, and vulnerable population considerations.`
42. `Create an informed consent form for [study type]. Language: readable (8th grade level). Include: purpose, procedures, risks, benefits, confidentiality, voluntary participation, contact information, and right to withdraw.`
43. `Develop a data management plan for [research project]. Include: data types, collection, storage (security), backup, sharing (repositories), retention, and destruction schedule. Align with funder requirements.`
44. `Write a research ethics case study about [scenario]. Present: the dilemma, stakeholders, ethical principles at play (beneficence, autonomy, justice, non-maleficence), analysis, and recommended action.`
45. `Create a conflict of interest disclosure statement for [research context]. Identify: potential conflicts (financial, intellectual, personal), mitigation strategies, and transparency measures.`
